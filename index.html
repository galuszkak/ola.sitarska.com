Hey, I'm Ola, a software developer & hobbyist event organizer living in Warsaw, Poland.

I started fiddling with code when I was 14. Very quickly this new hobby became my passion and lifestyle. I am an entrepreneur that loves to make meaningful things and sell them to people. I enjoy making things simpler and joyful to use. I love coding -- that's why I teached myself a variety of languages: Python, JavaScript, C and PHP. I enjoy Python and Django the most and I am honoured to be a Django Software Foundation developer member. I dig in some hardware-arduino-3dprinting stuff too.

Entrepreneurship runs in my blood and as a result of that I created my first successful online business when I was 16. I am not ashamed to say that I failed a couple of times, too -- these were probably the most learning experiences in my life. Currently I'm a co-founder of Issue Stand, where we make a magazine publishers' life easier. I was also named one of the Top 100 Women in Tech in Europe on TechCrunch.

I love creating amazing experiences for people I care about. I organize community events for developers because, apparently, I am awesome at this. Together with Kuba, we did HACKWAW three times so far. We also organized DjangoCon Europe in a crazy circus tent on a horse racing track and transformed 300 people into Makers in a weekend. Today I am focused on spreading Make Things all over world and teaching women about programming by co-founding Django Girls. This work is purely non-profit and chartiable so far but feels totally awesome.

Personally, I believe in karma, love spring, patterns and berries. I live with Wojtek and our two cats in Warsaw. I enjoy hard challenges, big changes and I try to live by this rules.
